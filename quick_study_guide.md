### Unit 12: Real Estate Financing ‚≠ê‚≠ê (Heavy Math)
**Critical Concepts:**
- **Loan types:**
  - Conventional (not government-backed)
  - FHA (government-insured)
  - VA (government-guaranteed)
- **Mortgage components:**
  - Principal
  - Interest
  - Taxes
  - Insurance (PITI)
- **Key terms:**
  - Amortization
  - Points (1 point = 1% of loan amount)
  - Loan-to-value ratio (LTV)
  - Down payment

**Math Formulas:**
- **Monthly Payment** = Loan Amount √ó Factor (from amortization table)
- **Total Interest** = (Monthly Payment √ó Number of Payments) - Loan Amount
- **LTV Ratio** = Loan Amount √∑ Property Value
- **Points in Dollars** = Loan Amount √ó (Points √∑ 100)

**Example Problem:**
- Loan: $125,000 at 6.68%
- Monthly payment: $1,085
- Total payments over 30 years: $1,085 √ó 360 = $390,600
- Total interest: $390,600 - $125,000 = $265,600

**Tip:** 15-year loans have higher monthly payments but significantly lower total interest!

### Unit 16: Real Estate Appraisal
**Key Concepts:**
- **Three approaches to value:**
  1. Sales comparison approach (most common for residential)
  2. Cost approach (new construction, special purpose)
  3. Income approach (investment/rental property)
- **Principles of value:**
  - Supply and demand
  - Substitution
  - Conformity
  - Contribution
  - Highest and best use
- **Types of depreciation:**
  - Physical deterioration
  - Functional obsolescence
  - External (economic) obsolescence

**Formula:** Value = Net Operating Income √∑ Cap Rate

## üìù Study Tips

### For Vocabulary:
- Focus on "Key Terms" at beginning of each unit

- Memorize key formulas
- Practice problems multiple times
- Use calculator efficiently
- Show your work to catch errors
- Check if answer makes sense

### For Concepts:
- Understand WHY, not just WHAT
- Use real-world examples
- Create visual diagrams for complex relationships
- Teach concepts to someone else

### Practice Questions Strategy:
1. Read question carefully - identify what's being asked
2. Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
3. Watch for absolute words (always, never) - usually wrong
4. Look for qualifiers (except, not, unless)
5. When unsure, go with first instinct
