## Key Terms

- **Associate Licensee**: A licensed real estate professional working under a broker
- **Appreciation**: Increase in property value over time
- **Broker**: Licensed individual who can operate independently and employ sales associates
- **Capital Gain**: Profit from the sale of a capital asset
- **Depreciation**: Decrease in property value over time
- **Equity Buildup**: Gradual increase in ownership interest as mortgage is paid down
- **Exchanges**: Trading one property for another (like-kind exchanges)
- **Investing**: Purchasing property for financial return
- **Leverage**: Using borrowed money to increase purchasing power
- **Liquidity**: Ease of converting an asset to cash
- **Market**: Economic environment where buyers and sellers interact
- **Real Estate Licensee**: Anyone licensed to practice real estate
- **Salesperson/Sales Associate**: Licensed individual working under a broker
- **Supply and Demand**: Economic forces that determine pricing

## Key Concepts

### Real Estate Careers
- Real estate brokers and sales associates
- Appraisers
- Property managers
- Real estate educators
- Mortgage brokers and loan officers
- Real estate attorneys
- Home inspectors
- Title and escrow professionals

### Property Classifications
- Residential property
- Commercial property
- Industrial property
- Agricultural/rural property
- Special purpose property

### Supply and Demand Factors
- **Supply factors**: Construction costs, labor availability, government controls, land availability
- **Demand factors**: Population, employment, income levels, demographics, credit availability

### Real Estate Investment Advantages
- **Leverage**: Ability to control large asset with small down payment
- **Equity buildup**: Gradual ownership increase through mortgage payments
- **Appreciation**: Property value increase over time
- **Tax advantages**: Deductions for mortgage interest, property taxes, depreciation
- **Income generation**: Rental income potential
- **Hedge against inflation**: Real estate often appreciates with inflation

### Real Estate Investment Disadvantages
- **Liquidity**: Difficult to convert to cash quickly
- **Depreciation**: Property can decrease in value
- **Market fluctuations**: Values can be volatile
- **Management requirements**: Properties require ongoing maintenance
- **Transaction costs**: High costs to buy and sell
- **Economic sensitivity**: Affected by economic conditions

## Important Reminders

⚠️ Real estate is NOT a liquid investment - takes time to sell
⚠️ Appreciation is not guaranteed - markets can decline
⚠️ Multiple career paths exist beyond sales
⚠️ Supply and demand drive all market activity
