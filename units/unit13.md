## Federal Housing Administration (FHA)

### Overview

**FHA**: Created 1934 during Great Depression
- Part of HUD (Department of Housing and Urban Development)
- **Insures loans**, does NOT make loans
- Protects lenders against borrower default
- Encourages homeownership

### FHA Loan Characteristics

**Down Payment**:
- As low as **3.5%**
- Lower than conventional loans
- More accessible to first-time buyers

**Credit Requirements**:
- More flexible than conventional
- Lower credit scores accepted
- Recent bankruptcy/foreclosure more acceptable

**Loan Limits**:
- Vary by county
- Based on median home prices
- Updated annually
- Higher in high-cost areas

**Interest Rates**:
- Competitive market rates
- Often similar to conventional
- May be slightly higher or lower

### Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP)

**Required on all FHA loans**:

**Upfront MIP (UFMIP)**:
- Currently 1.75% of loan amount
- Usually financed into loan
- Paid at closing

**Annual MIP**:
- Paid monthly
- Amount varies by LTV and loan term
- Cannot be canceled in many cases
- Required for life of loan if down payment < 10%

**Example**:
- Loan: $200,000
- Upfront MIP: $200,000 × 0.0175 = $3,500

### Property Requirements

**Must meet FHA standards**:
- Property appraisal required
- Health and safety standards
- Habitability requirements
- Repairs may be required
- Well and septic inspections (if applicable)

**Eligible Properties**:
- 1-4 unit residences
- Condominiums (FHA-approved)
- Manufactured homes
- Must be owner-occupied

### FHA Loan Assumability

**FHA loans are assumable**:
- Buyer takes over seller's loan
- Must qualify
- Keeps original interest rate
- Valuable in rising rate environment

## Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) Loans

### Overview

**VA**: Guarantees loans for eligible veterans and service members
- **Guarantees loans**, does NOT make them
- Created by Servicemen's Readjustment Act (1944 GI Bill)
- Encourages homeownership for veterans

### Eligibility

**Who Qualifies**:
- Veterans with minimum service requirements
- Active-duty service members
- Certain National Guard and Reserve members
- Surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from service-related disability

**Certificate of Eligibility (COE)**:
- Proves eligibility
- Obtained from VA
- Required for loan application

### VA Loan Benefits

**No Down Payment** ⭐:
- 100% financing available
- Biggest advantage of VA loans
- Subject to lender approval and value

**No Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)**:
- No monthly insurance premium
- Saves significant money

**Competitive Interest Rates**:
- Often lower than conventional
- Guaranteed by government

**Flexible Credit Requirements**:
- No minimum credit score (lender may have minimum)
- More lenient than conventional

### VA Loan Costs

**VA Funding Fee**:
- One-time fee to VA
- Helps offset program costs
- Percentage varies by:
  - First-time vs. subsequent use
  - Down payment amount
  - Type of service (regular vs. reserves)
- Can be financed into loan
- **Waived for disabled veterans**

**Typical Funding Fees**:
- First time, no down payment: 2.3%
- First time, 5% down: 1.65%
- First time, 10%+ down: 1.4%
- Subsequent use: Higher percentages

**Example**:
- Loan: $250,000
- First-time use, no down payment
- Funding fee: $250,000 × 0.023 = $5,750

### VA Entitlement

**Entitlement**: Amount VA will guarantee
- Basic entitlement: $36,000
- Additional entitlement for higher-priced homes
- Can be restored after loan paid off
- Partial entitlement if previous loan not paid off

### Property Requirements

**Must meet VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs)**:
- Safe, sanitary, and sound
- VA appraisal required
- Termite inspection (most areas)
- Well and septic certification

**Certificate of Reasonable Value (CRV)**:
- VA appraisal establishing value
- Required before loan approval

### VA Loan Assumability

**VA loans are assumable**:
- Buyer must qualify
- Can be assumed by non-veterans
- Veteran's entitlement tied up until loan paid off
- Veteran should get release of liability

## USDA Rural Development Loans

### Overview

**USDA**: United States Department of Agriculture
- Promotes rural homeownership
- **Guarantees loans** to lenders
- Focus on rural and suburban areas

### Loan Features

**No Down Payment**:
- 100% financing possible
- Income limits apply
- Must meet eligibility

**Location Requirements**:
- Rural and some suburban areas
- Population under 35,000 typically
- USDA eligibility maps available
- More areas eligible than expected

**Income Limits**:
- Based on area median income
- Generally up to 115% of median
- Varies by location and family size

**Mortgage Insurance**:
- Upfront guarantee fee (1%)
- Annual fee (0.35%)
- Lower than FHA

## Federal Reserve System (The Fed)

### Role in Real Estate Financing

**Central bank of United States**:
- Controls monetary policy
- Influences interest rates
- Affects availability of credit

### Monetary Policy Tools

**1. Discount Rate**:
- Rate Fed charges member banks
- Influences other interest rates
- Higher rate = tighter money
- Lower rate = easier money

**2. Reserve Requirements**:
- Amount banks must keep in reserve
- Higher requirements = less lending
- Lower requirements = more lending

**3. Open Market Operations** (Most Important):
- Buying/selling government securities
- Buying = increases money supply (easier credit)
- Selling = decreases money supply (tighter credit)

### Effect on Real Estate

**Lower interest rates**:
- More affordable mortgages
- Increased buying power
- Stimulates real estate market

**Higher interest rates**:
- Less affordable mortgages
- Decreased buying power
- Slows real estate market

## Primary vs. Secondary Mortgage Markets

### Primary Market

**Where loans are originated**:
- Direct lender-borrower relationship
- Create new loans

**Participants**:
- Commercial banks
- Savings and loan associations
- Credit unions
- Mortgage bankers
- Mortgage brokers

**Mortgage Banker**:
- Originates loans with own funds
- Often sells loans to secondary market
- Services loans (collects payments)

**Mortgage Broker**:
- Brings borrowers and lenders together
- Does NOT lend own funds
- Paid finder's fee/commission

### Secondary Market

**Where loans are bought and sold**:
- Provides liquidity to primary market
- Allows lenders to make more loans

**Major Players**:

**Fannie Mae (FNMA)**: Federal National Mortgage Association
- Government-Sponsored Enterprise (GSE)
- Buys conventional loans
- Issues mortgage-backed securities
- Largest player in secondary market

**Freddie Mac (FHLMC)**: Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation
- Government-Sponsored Enterprise (GSE)
- Buys conventional loans
- Stabilizes mortgage market
- Works with smaller lenders

**Ginnie Mae (GNMA)**: Government National Mortgage Association
- Government agency (part of HUD)
- Guarantees securities backed by FHA/VA loans
- Only deals with government-backed loans
- Full faith and credit of U.S. government

### Conforming vs. Non-Conforming Loans

**Conforming Loans**:
- Meet Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac standards
- Loan limits (updated annually)
- Credit and documentation requirements
- Can be sold to secondary market

**Non-Conforming Loans**:
- Don't meet secondary market standards
- **Jumbo loans**: Exceed conforming limits
- **Subprime loans**: Lower credit quality
- Portfolio loans (kept by lender)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Thinking FHA/VA make loans (they insure/guarantee only)
❌ Confusing Fannie Mae and Ginnie Mae
❌ Forgetting VA loans have funding fee
❌ Believing only rural farms qualify for USDA (suburbs too!)
❌ Mixing up primary and secondary markets

## Key Comparisons

### FHA vs. VA Loans

| Feature | FHA | VA |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Backed by | Insurance | Guarantee |
| Down payment | 3.5% minimum | 0% possible |
| Mortgage insurance | MIP required | No PMI |
| Eligibility | Anyone qualified | Veterans only |
| Upfront fee | 1.75% MIP | 2.3% funding fee* |
| Annual fee | Yes | No |

*Waived for disabled veterans

### Fannie Mae vs. Freddie Mac vs. Ginnie Mae

| Entity | Type | Loans | Guarantees |
|--------|------|-------|-----------|
| Fannie Mae | GSE | Conventional | MBS |
| Freddie Mac | GSE | Conventional | MBS |
| Ginnie Mae | Government | FHA/VA/USDA | MBS |

## Key Terms to Memorize

- **FHA**: Federal Housing Administration (insures loans)
- **VA**: Department of Veterans Affairs (guarantees loans)
- **USDA**: Rural development loans
- **MIP**: Mortgage insurance premium (FHA)
- **Funding fee**: One-time VA fee
- **COE**: Certificate of Eligibility (VA)
- **CRV**: Certificate of Reasonable Value (VA appraisal)
- **Fannie Mae (FNMA)**: Buys conventional loans
- **Freddie Mac (FHLMC)**: Buys conventional loans
- **Ginnie Mae (GNMA)**: Guarantees FHA/VA securities
- **Conforming loan**: Meets secondary market standards
- **Jumbo loan**: Exceeds conforming limits
