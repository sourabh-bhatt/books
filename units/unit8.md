## Key Concepts

### Real Estate Brokerage

**Broker**: Licensed professional who can operate independently
- Can employ sales associates
- May own brokerage firm
- Higher level license than salesperson
- Additional education/experience required

**Salesperson/Sales Associate**: Licensed professional who must work under a broker
- Cannot operate independently
- License held by employing broker
- Must have written employment agreement

---

## Broker Responsibilities

### Supervision and Management
- Supervise all affiliated licensees
- Review contracts and documents
- Maintain proper records
- Ensure compliance with laws
- Provide training and oversight

### Trust Account Management
- Handle client funds properly
- Maintain separate trust/escrow account
- **Commingling** = illegal mixing of client and personal funds
- **Conversion** = illegal use of client funds

### Record Keeping
- Transaction files (typically 3-6 years, varies by state)
- Commission records
- Listing and sales contracts
- Disclosure forms
- Trust account records

## Employment Relationships

### Employee vs. Independent Contractor

**Employee Status**:
- Broker controls how work is performed
- Set hours and work location
- Regular salary or hourly wage
- Employer withholds taxes
- Receives employee benefits
- Workers' compensation coverage

**Independent Contractor Status**:
- Controls own work methods and schedule
- Commission-based compensation
- No tax withholding (receives 1099)
- No employee benefits
- Own expenses and overhead
- Must meet IRS three requirements:
  1. Licensed as real estate agent
  2. Substantially all compensation based on production, not hours
  3. Written agreement states independent contractor status

**Most real estate sales associates**: Independent contractors

## License Law Requirements

### License Levels

**Salesperson/Sales Associate**:
- Entry-level license
- Must work under broker
- Can list, sell, represent clients
- Cannot supervise others

**Broker**:
- Higher-level license
- Can work independently
- Can employ sales associates
- Additional education required
- Usually requires 2-3 years experience

**Managing Broker/Broker-in-Charge**:
- Responsible for office operations
- Supervises all licensees in office
- Ensures legal compliance

### License Requirements (vary by state)
- Pre-licensing education
- Pass state exam
- Background check
- Application and fees
- Continuing education for renewal

### License Display and Use
- License must be displayed in office
- Cannot practice without active license
- Must renew periodically
- Inactive/inactive status available

## Antitrust Laws

**Purpose**: Promote competition and prevent monopolies

### Sherman Antitrust Act (Federal)
Prohibits:
1. Contracts restraining trade
2. Monopolies

### Violations in Real Estate

**Price Fixing** ❌
- Competing brokers agree on commission rates
- Setting standard fees
- Most serious violation
- **ILLEGAL**: Two brokers agree "we'll all charge 6%"
- **LEGAL**: Individual broker sets own rates

**Group Boycotting** ❌
- Competitors agree to exclude others
- Refusing to work with certain brokers/companies
- **ILLEGAL**: Brokers agree not to show competitor's listings

**Market Allocation** ❌
- Dividing markets or customers
- Agreeing not to compete in areas
- **ILLEGAL**: Two brokers agree to split city and not compete

**Tie-In Arrangements** ❌
- Requiring purchase of unwanted service to get desired one
- **ILLEGAL**: Must use my mortgage company to get my listing services

### What IS Legal ✓
- Each company independently sets own rates
- Stating company's commission rate
- Negotiating commissions with clients
- Competing on service and value
- Offering different service levels

**Penalties**:
- Criminal prosecution
- Fines up to $1 million (corporations) or $100,000 (individuals)
- Prison sentences
- Civil lawsuits (treble damages - 3× actual damages)

## Professional Organizations

### National Association of REALTORS® (NAR)
- **REALTOR®**: Trademarked term for NAR members
- Must follow Code of Ethics
- Membership voluntary
- Provides education and advocacy

### Code of Ethics
- Standards of professional conduct
- Duties to clients, public, and other REALTORS®
- Enforceable through NAR grievance process
- Violations can result in fines or expulsion

### Multiple Listing Service (MLS)
- Database of properties for sale
- Members share listings
- Facilitates cooperation
- Increases property exposure

## Brokerage Business Models

### Types of Brokerages

**Traditional Full-Service**:
- Complete range of services
- Higher commission splits to firm
- More support and resources

**Discount Brokerage**:
- Lower commissions
- Limited services
- Technology-focused

**100% Commission**:
- Agents keep all commission
- Pay monthly fees or per-transaction fees
- Minimal support from broker

**Teams**:
- Agents work together under one brand
- Pool resources and leads
- Share commissions within team

## Trust Funds and Escrow Accounts

### Trust Account Rules

**Requirements**:
- Separate account for client funds
- Cannot mix with broker's funds (commingling)
- Detailed records required
- Regular reconciliation
- Interest (varies by state law)

**What Goes in Trust Account**:
- Earnest money deposits
- Tenant security deposits
- Client funds held for others
- Advance fee payments

**What Does NOT Go in Trust Account**:
- Broker's commission (after earned)
- Broker's operating expenses
- Personal funds (except minimal amount for bank fees)

**Commingling**: Illegally mixing client and broker funds
- Serious violation
- Can result in license suspension/revocation

**Conversion**: Illegally using client funds
- Criminal act
- License revocation
- Criminal prosecution possible

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Thinking REALTOR® = real estate licensee (REALTOR® is NAR member only)
❌ Believing all licensees are independent contractors (can be employees)
❌ Confusing price fixing with setting your own commission
❌ Mixing up commingling and conversion
❌ Forgetting salesperson must work under broker

## Key Comparisons

### Employee vs. Independent Contractor

| Factor | Employee | Independent Contractor |
|--------|----------|----------------------|
| Control | Broker controls how work done | Agent controls own methods |
| Hours | Set by broker | Flexible |
| Compensation | Salary or hourly | Commission-based |
| Taxes | Withheld by broker | Agent pays (1099) |
| Benefits | May receive | None from broker |
| Status | Less common in real estate | Most common |

### Legal vs. Illegal Under Antitrust

| Legal ✓ | Illegal ❌ |
|---------|-----------|
| Set your own commission rate | Agree with competitors on rates |
| Compete on services | Boycott other firms |
| Market your unique value | Divide up territory with competitors |
| Offer package deals | Require tie-in services |
